SHAHID AFRIDI

Big Man Big Game

Here's a time line of Afridi's career:

  • 2 October 1996 - Made international debut in ODI against Kenya.
  • 4 October 1996 - He became the youngest batsman at 16 years and 217 days to score an international century. On his batting debut against Sri Lanka, he scored the fastest ever century at the time in just 37 balls hitting eleven sixes in the process, which is also a record.
  • 1998 - He made his test debut two years later, picking five wickets in the first innings against Australia.
  • 2005 - In 2005, he helped Pakistan beat India by scoring a quick-fire half century and also by picking 5 wickets. In the same year, Afridi scored another fastest century from just 45 balls.
  • 2006 - His T20I debut was on 28 August 2006 against England.
  • 2007 - Afridi also became the first player to receive the Man of the Series Award in the first T20 World Cup in 2007.
  • 2009 - Played an important role for Pakistan Cricket Team to bring home their first ever T20 worlducp TrophyHe was the highlight of the 2009 T20 World cup, where Pakistan beat Sri Lanka thanks to Afridi’s half-century. Afridi became the captain of his national side for the first time in 2009
  • 2010 - In July 2010, Afridi announced his retirement from Test cricket.
  • 2011 - He led the Pakistan team in the 2011 Cricket World Cup where they reached the semi-finals.
  • 2014 - In 2014 asia cup, Afridi played herioc knock against arch rivals India and helped Pakistan to win the game by hitting 2 sixes on consecutive balls in the last over.
  • 2015 - After the 2015 ICC WORLDCUP, Afridi retired from ODI cricket.
  • 2017 - In Feb 2017, he announced his retirement from all forms of international cricket

Overall Records:

Bowling-wise Afridi, who considers himself a better bowler than batter, has taken 395 wickets in ODI and 48 Test wickets. In addition he has also taken 98 T20I wickets.
Moreover, he holds the record for:

  • 2nd Most sixes in career (476)
  • 7th Most player-of-the-match awards (43)
  • 4th Most player-of-the-match awards (32) in One-Day Internationals
  • 1st Youngest player to score a hundred (16y 217d)
  • Most sixes in career1st Most sixes in career (351) in One-Day Internationals
